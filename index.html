You Can Now Just Talk to Robots — NVIDIA Just Made That Real
現在你可以直接「跟機器人說話」了

There's something that always bugged me about robots: they were only as smart as the programmer behind them. You had to painstakingly code every single movement, every edge case, every "what if the box is slightly tilted" scenario. That changes now.

This week, during National Robotics Week, NVIDIA dropped something I genuinely think is a landmark moment: the Isaac GR00T open model suite. The headline capability? You talk to the robot, and it figures out how to move.

Using what NVIDIA calls vision language action reasoning, these new GR00T models let robots receive natural language instructions — like "pick up the red cup from the table" — and translate that into actual physical movement. No line-by-line programming. No rigid scripts. Just plain language.

→ Newton 1.0 is now publicly available — an open-source physics engine (built with Google DeepMind and Disney Research!) that gives robots a realistic sense of how objects behave in the real world. Accurate collision detection, dexterous manipulation — the foundations robots need to handle the messy reality around them.

→ Isaac Sim 6.0 & Isaac Lab 3.0 let developers test robot behaviors in simulated environments before sending them into the real world. Virtual crashes over real ones.

→ Real-world applications are already live. Companies are using this for surgical robotics, agricultural harvesting, solar panel installation, and humanoid systems.

Here's why this matters to all of us: there's a global labor shortage estimated at over 50 million people. Roles that are dangerous, repetitive, or simply unfillable. Language-driven robots that can be instructed like a coworker — rather than programmed like a machine — could be a genuine answer to that.

Will they replace human workers? Some, yes — particularly in physical, repetitive jobs. But the bigger picture NVIDIA is painting is one where robots work alongside us, handling tasks humans can't or won't do.

I don't think we're ready for the speed at which this is moving. But I'm watching it very closely.

你有沒有想過，有一天你可以對機器人說：「幫我把那個紅色杯子從桌上拿過來」，然後它就直接去做了？

這不再是科幻電影的情節了。

上週在全國機器人週（National Robotics Week）期間，NVIDIA發布了一套叫做 Isaac GR00T 的開放模型，讓機器人終於能「聽懂人話」，並轉化成實際的物理動作。

過去，要讓機器人做一件事，工程師必須寫出每一個細節的程式碼——每個動作、每個邊界情況、每一種「盒子稍微傾斜時怎麼辦」的場景。而現在，透過NVIDIA所說的「視覺語言動作推理（Vision Language Action Reasoning）」，你只需要告訴機器人你想要什麼。

幾個關鍵發布：

→ Newton 1.0 正式開源——這是一個物理引擎（Physics Engine），由NVIDIA與Google DeepMind、Disney Research合作開發，讓機器人能真正理解現實世界中物體的物理行為，包括精確的碰撞偵測與靈巧操作能力。

→ Isaac Sim 6.0 與 Isaac Lab 3.0 讓開發者可以先在虛擬環境中測試機器人的行為，再讓它進入真實世界——在虛擬世界摔壞，總比在現實中好。

→ 已有多個真實應用落地，包括手術機器人、農業採收、太陽能板安裝，以及人形機器人（Humanoid Robot）系統。

為什麼這件事對我們每個人都有意義？全球勞動力短缺超過五千萬個職缺，很多是危險、重複性高、或根本找不到人做的工作。能用自然語言（Natural Language）指令操控的機器人，就像在指導一位同事，而不是操作一台機器——這可能是真正的解答。

這個領域的發展速度，老實說讓我有點跟不上。但我會繼續密切關注。

AI Won the Math Olympics But Can't Read a Clock 50% of the Time
AI贏了數學奧林匹克，卻有一半時間看不懂時鐘

Stanford just released its 2026 AI Index Report, and I've been sitting with one stat ever since I read it: the best AI models in the world solve PhD-level physics questions and win at competition math — and correctly read an analog clock only 50.1% of the time.

That's basically a coin flip.

This is what researchers are calling the "Jagged Frontier" — and I think it's the most important concept in AI right now. We keep measuring AI by what it can do at its absolute best. We give it coding benchmarks, math exams, science tests — and it's acing all of them. SWE-bench coding scores jumped from 60% to nearly 100% in a single year. The best models now clear 50% on Humanity's Last Exam, a notoriously brutal test spanning every academic field.

But that same AI? Give it a photo of an old-fashioned analog clock and ask what time it is. Fifty-fifty chance it gets it wrong.

→ Household robots fare even worse. Success at folding clothes: just 12%. These are physical, real-world tasks — not abstract reasoning puzzles.

→ AI transparency is actually going backwards. The Foundation Model Transparency Index score dropped from 58 to 40. The most capable companies are now revealing less about how their models work, what they're trained on, and what the risks are.

→ The job market is already shifting. US software developers aged 22 to 25 have seen employment drop nearly 20% since 2024. Entry-level work is being hit first.

→ The US-China AI gap has nearly vanished. As of March 2026, US models lead Chinese counterparts by just 2.7 percentage points. And the flow of AI researchers coming into the US has dropped 89% since 2017.

There's also a striking 50-point divide between AI experts (73% positive about AI's job impact) and the general US public (just 23% positive). That gap should concern us.

What I take from all this: AI is genuinely remarkable — and genuinely incomplete. We're deploying it faster than we understand it, with companies disclosing less just as the stakes get higher. The jagged frontier isn't a bug we'll patch soon. It's a fundamental feature of how this technology works right now. Knowing where it's jagged matters enormously.

基本上就是靠骰子決定。

研究人員把這個現象叫做「鋸齒形前沿（Jagged Frontier）」——我覺得這是目前AI領域最重要的概念之一。我們總是用AI最好的表現來評估它：程式設計基準測試（SWE-bench）、數學考試、科學測試——它都在快速進步。程式生成能力一年內從60%跳到接近100%。最強的模型在「人類最後的考試（Humanity's Last Exam）」這個涵蓋所有學術領域的超難測試中，正確率超過50%。

但是，給它看一張老式時鐘的照片，問它幾點？五五波。

幾個讓我印象深刻的數字：

→ 家用機器人的表現更糟。 折衣服的成功率只有12%。這些是現實世界中的動作任務，不是抽象的推理問題。

→ AI透明度正在下降。 基礎模型透明度指數（Foundation Model Transparency Index）從58分降到40分。最強大的公司，反而對自己的模型揭露越來越少——訓練資料、風險評估、能力說明，全都越來越不透明。

→ 就業市場已在改變。 22至25歲的美國軟體開發工程師，自2024年以來就業率下降了近20%。入門級職缺首當其衝。

→ 美中AI差距幾乎消失。 截至2026年3月，美國AI模型領先中國的差距只剩2.7個百分點。而且自2017年以來，流入美國的AI研究人員減少了89%——這是個結構性的隱憂。

還有一個令人擔憂的50點落差：73%的AI專家認為AI對就業市場的影響是正面的，但只有23%的一般美國民眾這樣認為。這個落差值得我們深思。

我對這份報告的感受是：AI的能力確實令人驚嘆——但它同時也是真實地不完整。我們部署它的速度，比我們理解它的速度還快。公司披露的資訊越來越少，偏偏這個時候風險反而越來越高。

「鋸齒形前沿」不是一個短期能修補的缺陷，而是這項技術在當下運作方式的根本特質。知道它在哪裡「鋸齒」，至關重要。

Google just made AI 6× cheaper to run — without losing a single bit of quality
Google 剛讓 AI 運行成本降低 6 倍——而且沒有犧牲任何一點品質

Here's something that doesn't get enough attention: one of the biggest barriers to AI being everywhere is simply memory. Running a large language model requires an enormous amount of RAM — which is why it still costs so much to run AI at scale, and why truly smart AI on your phone feels far away. Google may have just changed that equation. Their research team unveiled something called TurboQuant at ICLR 2026 — a new algorithm that compresses the "working memory" of AI models (technically called the KV cache) from 16 bits down to just 3 bits. Think of it like discovering a much better ZIP format specifically for AI's brain.
